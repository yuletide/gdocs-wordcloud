Design Challenge process

Day 1 they’ll have access to SEPTA, PHA officials and residents, Educators, and people from City Govt.

  1. They do a speed dating process so students can talk to each of these groups/people
  2. If we’re there during this part, move around and listen in on speed dating sessions

Day 2 and 3 are immersion/observation in the community around Temple

  1. 3-6 businesses will be open for questions
  2. they observe complex landscapes
  3. PHA sites
  4. Use a checkin app from LavaHound, which is customized with info about each checkin spot

Day 4 they move from seeing to doing. We’ll be there for 2nd ½ of day. It’s a structured process that includes timed responses to these questions:

  1. What inspires you? about what they’ve seen
  2. Who are the stakeholders? who’s affected by the thing(s) that inspire you. They construct a persona at this point. Be sure to keep reminding them to return to their persona throughout the other exercises.
  3. What are the stakeholders unmet needs? Why are these important - what problems are the needs solving
  4. What solutions do you propose? We’ll be there for this. James asked us to help them see the potential of technology to their solution(s).
  5. How is the solution sustainable? how will it integrate into the things people do? and how is it economically sustainable - eg if its supported by grant funding, this isn’t sustainable.

Day 5 lunch, judging, prizes[a]

After the challenge, students put everything they’ve gathered and made on a USB and this is given to the people working in the Design phase (and the hackathon).
